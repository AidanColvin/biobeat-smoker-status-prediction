# Rubric

## Problem Description (20 points)

- **Clarity of the problem statement (5 points)**  
  The student clearly articulates the problem being solved by the competition.

- **Dataset description (5 points)**  
  Detailed description of the dataset including sample size, number of features, and the response variable.  
  The meaning of each feature/predictor is clearly explained.

- **Objective (5 points)**  
  Clearly states the objective of the competition and what success looks like (e.g., the metric(s) used to evaluate submissions).

- **Relevance to field (5 points)**  
  Briefly discusses the relevance and potential impact of solving the problem in the context of health or other scientific fields.

---

## Methodology (30 points)

- **Overview of approaches (10 points)**  
  Describes all methods attempted during the project, including both those covered in class and those learned independently.

- **Rationale for chosen method (10 points)**  
  Explains why the final method was chosen over others, including considerations of its suitability for the problem, dataset, and any constraints (e.g., computational resources).

- **Implementation details (5 points)**  
  Provides enough detail about the implementation (e.g., data preprocessing, model selection, hyperparameter tuning) to understand how the project was carried out.

- **Reproducibility (5 points)**  
  Includes details (e.g., code snippets, software versions) that would allow someone else to reproduce the work.

---

## Results and Evaluation (25 points)

- **Performance metrics (10 points)**  
  Clearly presents the results using appropriate performance metrics.  
  Includes a discussion on the choice of metrics.

- **Analysis of results (10 points)**  
  Offers a thorough analysis of the results, including strengths and weaknesses of the model(s).  
  Discusses any interesting findings or unexpected outcomes.

- **Comparative analysis (5 points)**  
  If applicable, compares the performance of the chosen method against other methods attempted or baseline models, using clear figures/tables/numbers.

---

## Presentation and Writing Quality (15 points)

- **Organization and structure (5 points)**  
  The report is well-organized, with a logical flow that makes it easy to follow.

- **Clarity of writing (5 points)**  
  The writing is clear, concise, and free of grammatical or spelling errors.  
  Technical terms are adequately explained.

- **Visuals and tables (5 points)**  
  Effectively uses figures, tables, and charts to illustrate findings.  
  Visuals are clear, well-labeled, and appropriately referenced in the text.

---

## Creativity and Innovation (10 points)

- **Novelty of approach (5 points)**  
  Demonstrates creativity or innovation in the approach to solving the problem, such as trying new methods, creatively preprocessing data, or inventing a novel application of a known method.

- **Insightfulness (5 points)**  
  Shows depth of insight in analyzing the problem and interpreting the results.  
  Offers thoughtful considerations on the implications of the findings for the field of biostatistics.
